Sim-to-Real VLA Pipeline for Mobile ALOHA

Undergraduate Research · ACAI Lab · January 2026 – Present
Python MuJoCo Jacobian IK LeRobot v3.0 dm_control VLA Models NSF HPC

Overview

  • Research conducted at the Advanced Computation and Artificial Intelligence (ACAI) Lab under Dr. Shengfeng Yang.
  • Training Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models for robotic manipulation requires thousands of demonstrations. Collecting this data physically is slow and expensive.
  • We built a MuJoCo digital twin of the 17-DOF Mobile ALOHA platform to generate large-scale training data autonomously.
  • The simulation provides multi-camera visual data (overhead + wrist views) and 14-DOF joint feedback across thousands of randomized episodes.

My contributions

  • Kinematics & Policy Development: Engineered four custom scripted policies for bimanual tasks (tube-picking and mid-air handovers) using a Jacobian-based damped least squares IK solver.
  • Advanced Manipulation Features: Implemented tube-position feedback to compensate for pendulum swing, grasp verification, and descent collision detection.
  • Domain Randomization: Built a procedural system to generate randomized distractor objects (plates, markers, blocks) across the workspace, ensuring the VLA model generalizes rather than memorizes.
  • Data Pipeline & Quality Control: Built a dense episode scoring system (Perfect to Catastrophic) and exported multi-modal data into LeRobot v3.0 format for model training.

Results

  • Pipeline successfully generates large-scale, standardized datasets recording joint states, velocities, efforts, and action commands.
  • Data is being used to fine-tune pre-trained VLA models via NSF ACCESS HPC resources.
  • Significantly reduced dependency on manual human teleoperation for the real-world Mobile ALOHA platform.
"Domain randomization isn't just an ML trick — it's how you teach a robot that the world doesn't look the same twice."

USA Blind Hockey Smart Puck

EPICS MOBI · Design Lead · August 2024 – May 2025
Fusion 360 KiCad LTspice Polyurethane 3D Printing PCB Design

Overview

  • Project developed in partnership with USA Blind Hockey through the EPICS Mobility program at Purdue.
  • Current blind hockey pucks use metal ball bearings for sound — they're heavy, injure players, and break after a single game.
  • Goal: design a reusable, safer, more affordable puck using electronic sound generation instead of physical bearings.
  • The puck had to survive the intense physical demands of an actual hockey game.
🏒 Replace with: snap-fit CAD design (Figure 4 from your PDF)

Figure 4: Early CAD iteration of the snap-fit puck design.

🟡 Replace with: physical polyurethane prototype photo (Figure 5 from your PDF)

Figure 5: Final 3D-printed polyurethane prototype showing the internal cavity.

My contributions

  • Directed a 5-member team as Design Lead throughout the full project cycle.
  • Electrical System: Designed the PCB layout in KiCad and ran circuit simulations in LTspice. Key challenge was making fragile electronics survive severe impacts.
  • Mechanical Design: Used Fusion 360 to run structural analysis and identify weak points. Iterated from a snap-fit design to a threaded closure for easier battery replacement and better durability.
  • Geometry Optimization: Drop tests revealed sharp angles caused stress concentrations. Redesigned with curved edges and fillets to better distribute impact forces.
  • Material Selection: Tested PETG, ultimately selected Polyurethane for superior impact resistance and energy absorption.
  • Conducted real-user testing with an actual hockey player to validate durability, sound output, and waterproofing.

Results

  • Final Polyurethane prototype withstood 500 N of force during drop testing.
  • Maintained waterproofing and consistent sound output throughout testing.
  • Delivered a functional proof-of-concept for a safer, reusable, multi-game blind hockey puck.
🥇 Validated with real athlete · 500N impact resistance · Waterproof
"We tested with an actual hockey player. Watching someone use something you made, and seeing it work, is a different kind of validation than any stress test."

Purdue University Milestone Course · Dassault Systèmes
CATIA 3DEXPERIENCE Apple Vision Pro Kinematics AR/VR Digital Twin

Overview

  • Designed a complete dual-piston mechanism from scratch and deployed it as an interactive AR digital twin on Apple Vision Pro.
  • Used the Dassault Systèmes 3DEXPERIENCE platform (Part Design, Assembly Design, Creative Experience).
  • Components include: manual hand-crank wheel, two-gear power transmission, main crankshaft, connecting rods, two piston heads, and cylinder block.
